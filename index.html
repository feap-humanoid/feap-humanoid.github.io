FEAP: FiLM-Enhanced Encoders and Adversarial Motion Priors for Humanoid Locomotion

Han Jiang, Teng Chen*, Guoteng Zhang, Member, IEEE,
Yibin Li, Member, IEEE, Yong Fan,
Xuewen Rong*, Senior Member, IEEE

School of Control Science and Engineering, Shandong University, Jinan, 250062, China
School of Rail Transportation, Shandong Jiaotong University, Jinan, Shandong, China

Abstract

Humanoid robots possess substantial potential for operation in complex environments, yet existing locomotion policies are often limited to simplified scenarios and suffer from poor robustness and inter-limb coordination. We introduce FEAP (FiLM-Enhanced Encoders and Adversarial Motion Priors), a reinforcement learning framework that enables adaptive and reliable humanoid locomotion on challenging terrains using only proprioceptive information. FEAP leverages a modular observation encoder that fuses multi-modal sensory inputs via Feature-wise Linear Modulation (FiLM), capturing both raw kinematics and high-level semantic features. To promote natural and energy-efficient movements, a parameterized adversarial motion prior based on a Wasserstein formulation provides a tunable inductive bias, allowing dynamic trade-offs between style fidelity and task performance. Safety is further ensured through constraint-aware policy optimization that penalizes unsafe behaviors. Trained in simulation with domain randomization and directly deployed on the Taishan Walker E1 humanoid robot, FEAP enables the robot to perform humanlike locomotion, exhibiting running on slopes and uneven terrains as well as walking over stair-like obstacles, while demonstrating superior robustness, adaptability, and safety compared to baseline methods.

Key Innovations

  • FiLM-Enhanced Encoders: Modular multi-modal encoders fused via FiLM for robust terrain inference from proprioception.
  • Parameterized Adversarial Motion Prior (AMP): Wasserstein-based AMP with tunable style-reward parameters to balance humanlike motion and task performance.
  • Safety-Constrained One-Stage Training & Real-World Deployment: Constraint-aware optimization and successful deployment on the Taishan Walker E1 robot across diverse terrains.
