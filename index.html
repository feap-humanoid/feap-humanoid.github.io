FEAP: FiLM-Enhanced Encoders and Adversarial Motion Priors for Humanoid Locomotion

Han Jiang*, Teng Chen, Guoteng Zhang, Xuewen Rong, Yibin Li
School of Control Science and Engineering, Shandong University, Jinan, China
*Indicates Equal Contribution
Paper Supplementary Code arXiv

Demonstration of humanoid robot performing walking, running, and stair climbing using FEAP.

Abstract

Humanoid robots possess substantial potential for operation in complex environments, yet existing locomotion policies are often limited to simplified scenarios and suffer from poor robustness and inter-limb coordination. We introduce FEAP (FiLM-Enhanced Encoders and Adversarial Motion Priors), a reinforcement learning framework that enables adaptive and reliable humanoid locomotion on challenging terrains using only proprioceptive information. FEAP leverages a modular observation encoder that fuses multi-modal sensory inputs via FiLM, capturing both raw kinematics and high-level semantic features. To promote natural and energy-efficient movements, a parameterized adversarial motion prior based on a Wasserstein formulation provides a tunable inductive bias, allowing dynamic trade-offs between style fidelity and task performance. Safety is further ensured through constraint-aware policy optimization that penalizes unsafe behaviors. Trained in simulation with domain randomization and directly deployed on the Taishan Walker E1 humanoid robot, FEAP enables the robot to perform humanlike locomotion, exhibiting running on slopes and uneven terrains as well as walking over stair-like obstacles, while demonstrating superior robustness, adaptability, and safety compared to baseline methods.

Methods & Contributions

  • FiLM-Enhanced Encoders: Modular observation encoder leveraging FiLM for adaptive feature fusion, capturing raw kinematics and high-level semantic features while inferring latent terrain characteristics.
  • Parameterized AMP: Uses Wasserstein distance to stabilize adversarial motion prior training and dynamically adjusts style rewards for humanlike motion with terrain adaptability.
  • Safety-Constrained One-Stage Training & Deployment: Single-stage training with safety constraints; deployed on Taishan Walker E1 robot demonstrating robust and humanlike locomotion over complex terrains.
