FEAP: Feature-Enhanced Encoders and Adversarial Motion Priors for Humanoid Locomotion

Han Jiang, Teng Chen*, Guoteng Zhang, Xuewen Rong, Yibin Li, Yong Fan

School of Control Science and Engineering, Shandong University, Jinan, 250062, China

Abstract

Humanoid robots possess great potential for operation in complex environments, yet existing locomotion policies are often limited to simplified scenarios and suffer from poor robustness and inter-limb coordination. We introduce FEAP (Feature-Enhanced Encoders and Adversarial Motion Priors), a reinforcement learning framework that enables adaptive and reliable humanoid locomotion on challenging terrains using only proprioceptive information. FEAP leverages a modular observation encoder that fuses multi-modal sensory inputs via feature-wise linear modulation (FiLM), capturing both raw kinematics and high-level semantic features. To promote natural and energy-efficient movements, a parameterized adversarial motion prior based on a Wasserstein formulation provides a tunable inductive bias, allowing dynamic trade-offs between style fidelity and task performance. Safety is further ensured through constraint-aware policy optimization that penalizes unsafe behaviors. Trained in simulation with domain randomization and directly deployed on the Taishan Walker E1 humanoid robot, FEAP enables the robot to perform humanlike locomotion, exhibiting humanlike running as well as walking over stair-like and uneven terrains, while demonstrating superior robustness, adaptability, and safety compared to baseline methods.

Key Innovations

  • Feature-Enhanced Encoders: A modular design with FiLM-based adaptive fusion that enables the recovery of latent terrain characteristics under partial observability.
  • Parameterized AMP: An adversarial motion prior stabilized by the Wasserstein distance and equipped with dynamic style reward adjustment for humanlike and adaptive locomotion.
  • Safety-Constrained One-Stage Training and Deployment: A single-stage training framework with safety constraints, successfully deployed on the Taishan Walker E1 humanoid robot, demonstrating robust and natural locomotion across diverse terrains.
